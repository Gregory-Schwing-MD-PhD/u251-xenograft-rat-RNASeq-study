\noindent To the Editor --- Laser interstitial thermal therapy (LITT) is increasingly deployed for deep-seated and recurrent glioblastoma, yet how the sublethal thermal penumbra reshapes the molecular state of cells that survive and seed recurrence remains poorly defined~\cite{LITT_review}. Most preclinical work has focused on cytoreductive efficacy and on transient blood--brain-barrier disruption~\cite{NagarajaLITT}; the cell-intrinsic transcriptional program of post-LITT residual disease has not been systematically described. To begin to close this gap, we performed bulk RNA-seq on matched primary (pre-LITT, $n=3$) and recurrent (post-LITT, $n=3$) tumors from a U251N orthotopic xenograft in athymic RNU/RNU rats treated with MRI-guided LITT in the previously described rat glioblastoma model~\cite{NagarajaLITT}, processed through xengsort cross-species read-sorting~\cite{xengsort} to isolate the human tumor transcriptome from host signal.

Primary and recurrent transcriptomes were globally distinct (Figure~\ref{fig1}A; PCA PC1 41.8\%, PC2 26.2\%), and the differentially expressed gene set (DESeq2; 35 genes at FDR\,$<$\,0.05, $|\log_{2}\mathrm{FC}|>1$; 23 up- and 12 down-regulated) was dominated by a coordinated translational-repression signature: small-sample GSEA (gene-set permutation, $n=3$ per arm) ranked eukaryotic translation elongation (NES\,$=-1.96$), the EIF2AK4 (GCN2) amino-acid-deficiency response (NES\,$=-1.95$), ribosome (NES\,$=-1.94$), and cellular-response-to-starvation (NES\,$=-1.93$) among the most depleted gene sets in the recurrent tumors (nominal $p<0.001$; FDR $q\approx0.15$--$0.25$) --- a coherent leading-edge signature of integrated-stress-response (ISR)-driven translational repression. This translation/ISR signature was paired with a coherent molecular-subtype reorganization --- a shift toward the Garofano Mitochondrial state (signature $z$ $-0.32\!\rightarrow\!+0.32$)~\cite{Garofano} and away from Neftel astrocyte-like ($+0.63\!\rightarrow\!-0.63$) and neural-progenitor-like ($+0.44\!\rightarrow\!-0.44$) programs~\cite{Neftel} --- consistent with a stress-adapted, metabolically reprogrammed persister population. Protein--protein interaction hubs in the differential set (\textit{BGN}, \textit{COL1A1}, \textit{IGFBP3}, \textit{CALB1}) further suggest extracellular-matrix and calcium-signaling remodeling around these residual cells.

We treated this signature as the target of an in-silico drug-repurposing pipeline (DSigDB GSEA~\cite{DSigDB}, with candidates reranked by an integrated score --- $|\mathrm{NES}|^{1.5}$ weighted by predicted blood--brain-barrier permeability --- that combines therapeutic enrichment magnitude with central-nervous-system penetrance). The top-ranked agents converged on two axes that are mechanistically coherent with translational shutdown under metabolic stress (Figure~\ref{fig1}B): the HIF/iron axis --- the iron-chelator/HIF-$1\alpha$ stabilizer ciclopirox (rank 1; integrated score 3.4) and the prolyl-hydroxylase inhibitor dimethyloxalylglycine (rank 3; 3.0) --- and the PI3K/mTOR pathway, where the canonical inhibitor LY-294002 ranked seventh (integrated score 2.4). We emphasize that this is a roadmap, not a result: the nominated agents are advanced for future preclinical interrogation as LITT adjuncts, not as validated therapeutics.

We acknowledge the following study limitations. The sample size ($n=3$ per arm) is small, the study uses a single cell-line xenograft, and no wet-lab functional or pharmacological validation is presented; the work is explicitly hypothesis-generating. Nevertheless, we believe the data warrant early reporting: the magnitude of the translation/ISR effect, its convergence with an interpretable subtype shift, and the mechanistic coherence of the nominated drug classes are unlikely to be incidental. As LITT moves further into front-line and salvage glioblastoma practice, defining the transcriptional state of post-thermal residual disease --- and prospectively designing combinatorial regimens that target it --- will be essential to extending the benefit of an otherwise minimally invasive cytoreductive modality.
